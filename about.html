Growing up with my time split between my parents in Alabama and Florida, I think I got lucky in the sense that my grandpa Ellis Champion - my partial eponym - probably encouraged me more mentally than physically (it probably doesn't hurt that I was the baby of my generation, too). After being impressed with my performance in school he would encourage me to continually grow and seek out knowledge after tossing a bit of money my way as a reward.


I like to think that getting a paycheck for what I do is the analogous sequence of events in adulthood, but this certainly set the stage for my work ethic and personal endeavors so far in life.



I think I can say I'm dedicated, but feel free to ask other people to be sure. I'm a software developer living in Huntsville, AL. Currently I'm working with Jacobs ISG and helping automate government business practices, which I think is pretty rad. After graduating with a BS in Computer Science from Auburn University, I stuck around for three years there working with Heartland Payment Systems designing and maintaining online and desktop payment processing systems. Things began to change as I encroached on a few life goals, so I decided to pick up and move to Huntsville to settle down for the forseeable future.


I like to describe myself in summary in three words:


Punctual

I strive to be on time, whether it's for an appointment or finishing a piece of work in an allotted timeframe. Punctuality was instilled in me as a very important concept from very early on in life. I view it as necessary to keep people happy, to meet goals, and to keep events relatively predictable. Even in fast-moving environments where requirements may change regularly and things become somewhat spontaneous punctuality keeps me grounded and focused on where I need to be and what needs to be done by when.

Meticulous

It's probably a good thing to be detail-oriented in a profession that relies on details. I try to create products that are the best they can be before handing them off to the next person in line or showing them off to others. Sometimes I'm satisfied with things relatively quickly, while other times this results in many, many rounds of changes before someone is able to look at a product of my work (don't look at the change history on this website, please). The desire to fine-tune things is in my nature. Aside from this, I tend to be nigh-obsessed with organization and routines. I find that the wildest of projects or requirements can be effectively handled after they are structured into to-do lists and run through a standard process.

Lighthearted

So long as work is being done and is meeting expectations, there's no need for things to be serious all the time. I enjoy making people laugh or smile, whether it's with me or at my expense (just not always at my expense - a person can only handle so much embarrassment). I don't ever like distracting people from their work, but in their downtime I'd rather them be happy than wound up or tense. Being goofy every now and again makes work a fun place to go and be, and not some mundane room where we spend the majority of our lives.
